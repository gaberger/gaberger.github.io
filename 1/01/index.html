Aristocrats, Artisans and Renovators

This is a story about my experiences in developing software over the years and the level of insanity it has become as open source has taken over the world.

In … Marc Andreeson had said software is eating the world. Many people were still clueless to what he meant by this but those of us who had been watching the writing on the wall knew very well he was talking about a shift in focus from closed, monolithic systems to a new world filled with creative options.

Back in the early 90’s software development outside of the major players required a vast amount of disipline, patience and fortitude. Unlike today, the Internet was still in its infancy. The Netscape browser the first real commercialized solution for businesses was x yrs old. Most businesses had limited access to the internet maybe a few DS1 cirtcuites running at 1.5Mbps or more common some v.56 dialup modem.

Gathering information was extermely difficult. GitHub didn’t exist note even stackoverflow.

Writing software is not a science. Regardless how we have tainted the vernacular like “computer science” or “software engineering” writing software takes a host of different skills from being a good translator to expressing an algorithem as a beautifully written recursive function.

Lets take a look at how people percieve software engineers.

We have the Aristrocrats. These are the noblest of software. They have spent time in some fine universities studying Object Oriented Languages, Propositional Logic, Formal Methods, lingustics. These are the the individuals who go through the rigor of category theory and are best at creating languages. Aristocrats can find their way through a problem very quickly but are always on the hunt for the most noblest game of distributed computing.

The Artisans are less formal. They usually have an innate sense of design, they may take a longer time to determine to get through the maze, but their creativity provides elegant solutions to complicated problems. (usually they only see the elegance however)

And finally the Renovators. These are the largest population of software pirates. They may have some higher learning from other insstitutions but leverage their tenacity and creativity to solve problems.
